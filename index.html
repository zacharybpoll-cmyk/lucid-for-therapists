How It Works

One app. Every clinician. No new workflow.

Each clinician in your practice installs Lucid independently. It runs locally on their Mac and never shares data across machines or clinicians.

01

Each clinician installs Lucid on their Mac

Five-minute setup per clinician. No shared server, no IT deployment, no EHR integration required. Each install is independent — data stays on each clinician's device.

Live same day

02

Lucid runs in the background during every session

Clinicians open Lucid before the session starts. It analyzes patient voice passively. By the time the session ends, a PHQ-9 and GAD-7 correlated assessment has been generated — along with the CPT documentation to bill it.

Zero session overhead

03

Patients check in 2-3 times per week between sessions

Each patient gets a check-in link. They speak into their browser for 60-90 seconds — no app, no account. Each check-in generates another billable assessment. 20 patients, 3 check-ins per week = 60 additional billing events per clinician, per week.

Patient Workflow

What your patients experience

Six simple steps. No behavior change required. Lucid works quietly in the background while your patients go about their day.

1

Download Lucid

Your patient downloads Lucid to their Mac. One installer, takes under a minute. No account creation, no email, no personal information collected.

2

Quick Setup

A 2-minute onboarding: grant microphone permission, enroll their voice with a short reading passage. That's it — Lucid is ready.

3

Just Talk Normally

Lucid listens passively throughout the day — calls, meetings, conversations. It analyzes voice patterns in real time and updates the dashboard with a composite Voice Score, clinical indicators (PHQ-9, GAD-7), and five voice dimensions.

4

Track Progress Over Time

Patients can view their trends — daily, weekly, monthly, or yearly. The Trends view shows their average score, best day, total readings, current streak, and clinical indicator trajectories.

5

Share with Their Therapist

When ready, the patient enters your pairing code in Settings. From that point forward, Lucid automatically sends encrypted pre-session reports before each appointment. The patient can revoke access at any time.

6

Complete Privacy

All voice processing happens entirely on the patient's device. No audio is ever transmitted, stored in the cloud, or accessible to anyone — including you. Only derived scores and patterns are shared, and only when the patient explicitly opts in.

Therapist Workflow

What you see as the provider

Objective voice data delivered before every session. Review in 60 seconds, bill two units, and walk into session with data-informed talking points.

1

Patient Connects

Give your patient a 5-character pairing code. They enter it in their Lucid app. You receive a confirmation that they've linked — no software to install on your end, no portal to manage.

2

Automatic Pre-Session Brief

Two hours before each scheduled appointment, you receive a Session Ready Brief via secure email. It's a single-page summary: Voice Score with 14-day trend, PHQ-9 and GAD-7 gauges, flagged patterns, and suggested conversation starters derived from the data.

3

60-Second Review

Scan the brief before your patient walks in. The layout is designed to read in under a minute: the Voice Score and trend tell you direction, the clinical gauges show severity, and the talking points give you an evidence-based way to open the conversation.

"Your stress score improved significantly since last session — that's real progress. But your data shows it's still much higher on weekdays than weekends. What's different about your weekdays right now?"
4

Data-Informed Sessions

Instead of relying solely on self-report, you have objective between-session data. Patients often under-report improvements or miss patterns they can't see. The data gives you a more complete picture and helps surface what matters most.

5

Clinical Report for Documentation

Access the full 3-page clinical report: Voice Score summary with confidence intervals, emotion profile analysis, linguistic markers table, notable patterns detected, and a complete billing documentation page with CPT code, outputs reviewed, and signature lines.

6

Bill CPT 96127 x 2

Each patient session generates two billable units of 96127 (Brief emotional/behavioral assessment). The documentation is pre-filled with assessment tool description, specific outputs reviewed, and clinical action checkboxes. Add your interpretation, sign, and submit.

Clinical Validation

Peer-reviewed. Double-blind. 14,898 patients.

The underlying voice biomarker model was evaluated in a cross-sectional study of 14,898 English-speaking adults in the US and Canada. Participants provided free-form speech (25+ seconds) and completed a self-reported PHQ-9.

At PHQ-9 cut-off score of 10 (moderate to severe depression), the model achieved sensitivity of 71.3% (95% CI: 69.0–73.5) and specificity of 72.5% (95% CI: 71.5–73.5). The study was conducted by Kintsugi Mindful Wellness and published January 2025.

The Trends view your clinicians use shows PHQ-9 and GAD-7 correlated scores across every session and check-in — continuous monitoring between appointments, not just a snapshot at session time.
